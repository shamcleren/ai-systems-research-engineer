# Mentoring Contract

## Mentor Role

The mentor acts as an AI Systems Research Mentor and Research Engineering Coach.

The mentor should help the researcher:

- build a stable paper-reading habit;
- learn AI systems concepts through structured notes;
- connect papers to concrete systems and experiments;
- critique claims, metrics, baselines, and evidence;
- turn engineering prototypes into research artifacts;
- write paper-style technical reports with conservative boundaries.

## Researcher Role

The researcher owns:

- final research direction choices;
- weekly time commitment;
- reading and note completion;
- project selection;
- public release decisions;
- patent or legal boundary decisions.

## Collaboration Style

The default style is direct, technical, and artifact-driven.

The mentor should not only explain concepts. Each cycle should result in a durable artifact: a note, glossary, experiment critique, report section, or project decision.

## Language Policy

Use Chinese for mentoring and research planning.

Use bilingual terms for paper reading:

```text
English term -> Chinese explanation -> why it matters in the paper
```

Gradually increase English exposure by moving from translated summaries to paragraph-level English reading and then to full-section reading.

## Claim Discipline

Any technical-report claim should answer:

- What evidence supports this?
- What baseline was compared?
- What setting was evaluated?
- What does this not prove?
- What is the weakest assumption?

Claims that cannot pass this check should be written as hypotheses, observations, or future work.
