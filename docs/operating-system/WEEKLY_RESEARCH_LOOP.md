# Weekly Research Loop

Use this loop for the first 12 weeks.

## Start-of-Week Rule

中文：每周开始时，先打开 `docs/todos/WEEK-N-TODO.md`，而不是直接读论文或写长期笔记。只有当 TODO 已经说明“从哪里开始、每一步读什么、有什么中文导读、答案写在本 step 哪里、完成后做什么”时，才开始执行。

English: At the start of each week, open `docs/todos/WEEK-N-TODO.md` first instead of directly reading the paper or writing long-term notes. Start execution only after the TODO explains where to start, what each step reads, what Chinese guidance is available, where the answer box is inside the step, and what to do after completion.

中文：如果 TODO 不满足这个标准，先按 `docs/operating-system/BEGINNER_TODO_SYSTEM.md` 和 `docs/templates/WEEKLY_TODO_TEMPLATE.md` 修 TODO，再开始学习任务。

English: If the TODO does not meet this standard, update it using `docs/operating-system/BEGINNER_TODO_SYSTEM.md` and `docs/templates/WEEKLY_TODO_TEMPLATE.md` before starting the learning task.

## Weekly Time Budget

Assume more than 12 hours per week.

Suggested split:

- 4 hours: main paper reading;
- 2 hours: lighter related reading;
- 2 hours: bilingual glossary and concept map;
- 2 hours: TRI experiment, critique, or design review;
- 2 hours: research note or technical-report writing.

## Weekly Outputs

Each week must produce:

1. One structured paper note.
2. One bilingual keyword table.
3. One TRI connection note.
4. One evaluation or claim-boundary critique.
5. One short research-writing artifact.

## Weekly Review Questions

End each week by answering:

- What did I understand this week that I could not explain last week?
- Which English terms became usable without translation?
- What research claim became clearer?
- What claim is still unsupported?
- Which TRI design or experiment should be questioned next?
- What should be read next week?

## Definition of Done

A week is complete only when the artifacts are written.

Watching videos, reading without notes, or discussing ideas without a written output does not complete the weekly loop.
